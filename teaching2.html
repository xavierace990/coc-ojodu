MATH.25:31-46

CHRISTIAN AND SOCIAL VICES

Right from time immemorial, human beings have always hide social vices. As children of God, we are expected to be different from the world and not be engaged in these vices. The purpose of this lesson is not for parents to all of sudden start beating our children when they get home but to be more strategic going forward and determine to raise Godly children forward.

1JOHN 2: 15 -17

COUPLES COOPERATION IN TRAINING CHILDREN

The church has not been putting much emphasis on proper training of our children. In some homes, this is done in an acquired culture brought in by civilization which is not in accord with Christians ethics (behaviour). Therefore, this topic at this time of our moral and spiritual decadence is important to all parents.

PROVERBS 22:6
The Joy of the Lord

Towards the close of His earthly ministry, Jesus did not assure His disciples of a life free from challenges in the cause of Christianity. And neither did He promise them earthly joy or happiness, but that is not to say that we don't need happiness in life as God's people, or work to acquire material things in life which give happiness. The joy Jesus Christ experienced in obedience to His Father; love for His Father; and faithfulness to His Father, was expected of His disciples then, as it is also demanded of christians today and always. This joy is perfect and perpetual, not earthly joy related to the acquisition or expectation of something good, though we need it.

John 15:11
SUBDUING THE ATTITUDE OF ANGER

Anger is a normal feeling and can be a positive emotion when it helps you work through issues or problem whether that's at work or at home. However, anger can become problematic if it leads to aggression, outburst or even physical altercations. Having the attitude to subdue anger is important for helping you avoid saying or doing something you may regret. Before anger escalates, you must be able to avoid some certain talk and argument most especially in the home, in the Church and our environs.

Eph.4:26-27

We live in uncertain times. The current socio-economic development has left millions of families grappling for survival. While a negligible few are living, the majority are just existing or surviving. The cost of living is skyrocketing with no end in sight while the standard of living is declining. Prices of goods and services are soaring. Criminality is on the rise. Morality has developed wings. Our long-cherished values are thrown overboard.

Rom. 5:3-5
